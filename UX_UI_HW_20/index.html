OVERVIEW

PROJECT DISCRIPTION Food for thought is a grocery app created to help individuals have control over their time, budget, and carbon footprint. The app allows users to choose options, whether users want to be sustainable or not. We feel the app will encourage individuals to start to be sustainable, one small step at a time.
THE PROBLEM Food For Thought was designed to save time by providing a more streamlined way to execute grocery shopping. We have observed that our product or service isn’t able to optimize the balance between affordable, health conscious, sustainable and local options, which is causing users to feel confused when trying to buy the best for their families.
THE GOAL A budget-forward grocery shopping app that provides users with in-depth product information in order to make healthier and more sustainable choices in a timely manner.

DISCOVER

USER RESEARCH User Research focuses on understaning the throught process consumers undergo when making grocery shopping decisions. We did a combination of 5 interviews and semi-structured surveys to know how users plan their grocery shopping. Together we brainstormed high-level questions based on our user persona to find out more about our users’ thought processes. Several key insight we discovered: They tend to go grocery shopping 1-3 times a week. Most participants are consious of budget, healthy options, and sustainability for grocery shopping. Sustainability was lower on the priority list, but when we had asked them what makes you motivated to practice sustainability while grocery shopping, each participant had a different definition for what sustainability meant to them (ex. reducing waste, buying local products, and to lessen my carbon footprint)
COMPETITOR RESEARCH
We gathered information about the competition in order to define the basic features of our application
What did we find?
The biggest competitors have the ability to provide weekly deals.
However, only some of them have health conscious and sustainable options for grocery shopping.
None of them have in-depth product information, such as where this product came from and how it was produced/ grown/ treated for the customers.
USER PERSONA Develop a clear understand of our target users.

IDEATION

PROBLEM STATEMENT Food For Thought was designed to achieve a more streamlined way to execute grocery shopping. We have observed that our product or service isn’t able to optimize the balance between affordable, health conscious and sustainable, which is causing our users to feel confused when trying to buy what is best for their families.
How might we improve Food For Thought so that our customers are successful in their grocery shopping based on their personal preferences and family needs?
MOTIVATION FOR USER

Reduce grocery shopping expense.

Have more positive impart on the enverionment.

Increase healthy eating habits

PROTOTYPE

USER FLOW For our user, we will prioritize how the app will be able to help with time management, price deals, and sustainability in order to fulfill our users' needs. We’ll have a pick up/ delivery option, daily deals, and a motivational reward system.
SKETCHING & WIREFRAMING DESIGN HIGH-FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

CONCLUSION

After doing our project, we noticed that users enjoy visuals that pop like our goals tab. We believe it’s because it is something that is exciting and engaging to them to keep themselves accountable, and results in users further using the application. We thought that in future designs we can provide rewards for following sustainable practices. Also, We learned that user research and usability test help a lot to get information about what the users needs and wants for the grocery shopping.
